Feeling hungry? Why not try these...

Cheap & Easy

Windsor’s High Street is full of small cafes and coffee shops. If you’re in the mood for something to-go, these are some great options: Costa, Greggs, Caffè Nero. There are a fair amount of small businesses in Windsor too, so it might be worth supporting: Monty’s Cafe, Lilly’s Cafe, or Millar’s Eatery.

Fairly Priced

If you’re after more of a main meal for lunch, or just something a little more “sit-down”, here’s my recommendation: Bill’s, Giggling Squid, or Wagamama. Here are some small businesses that you should also check out: Enzo’s, Sebastian’s, and Best Meze Grill. But Windsor has a multitude of options, so I suggest you take a stroll down the High Street and see what you fancy.

Splashing Out

Feel like you want to treat yourself and your friends to something a little more expensive? The Ivy is a brilliant place to dress up in your fancy attire and settle down in a beautifully decorated space. Some other high end recommendations I have: Côte Brasserie (situated on the riverside), Gilbey’s Bar, The Windsor Grill, Meat at the Parish, and À la Russe.

More in the mood for a drink?

Windsor & Eton Brewery

The Windsor & Eton Brewery is quite the hidden gem (if you like a good ale, that is). I’ve stopped off here a couple of times on my way back from a walk into the High Street; the interior atmosphere is so cosy, and the people are lovely. While you enjoy a drink here, you might even want to buy a bottle to take home with you.

Duchess of Cambridge

The Duchess of Cambridge is situated directly opposite the Castle, so if you can nab yourself a window seat, you can be guaranteed to have a nice view. You might fancy nursing a drink for a little while, once you get to the top of the High Street.

All Bar One

All Bar One is a lovely place to go for a lush cocktail and some sharing platters with your friends. Located directly next to the Windsor & Eton Central train station, it’s a nice way to take a break from your window shopping before you head back home.

(© Credit to Club Soda Guide for the image. It has been cropped slightly from the original.)

The Shambles Bar

Don’t worry, The Shambles won’t topple while you’re inside (despite how wonky it looks). This gem is located inside the Crooked House and is situated a tiny distance away from the castle. Reviews all around say that this is a friendly and welcoming place, so I recommend you stop by.
