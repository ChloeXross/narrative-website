The Obvious

Have you really been to Windsor if you haven't visited these places?


View Larger Map

Windsor Castle

Need I even mention this landmark? I'm sure the majority of people think of the Castle when they hear about Windsor, but if not, it's definitely worth a visit. There are some sections of the castle open to the public if you're curious to peek inside, but there are also some gorgeous gardens open during the summertime.

Located at the centre of everything bustling, you might want to get a bite to eat somewhere close by to keep it in your view. As well as this, there are plenty of lovely walks you can do to keep the castle in sight. In fact, let's take a look at one of them below...

The Long Walk

Ah, the Long Walk! 2.64 miles to be exact. It isn't always as bustling as this picture suggests, but it has been known to host a major event or two (such as a Royal Wedding or a Coronation).

Running from the Copper Horse (which you may have seen a picture of back on the Home Page) to the Castle, this stretch makes for the perfect afternoon stroll, or even a run if you're feeling up for it!

Legoland

Surprisingly, a lot of people I spoke to since moving to Manchester associate Windsor with Legoland. I used to go here a lot as a child, and I remember thoroughly enjoying it.

It’s a brilliant place to go if you have kids, or maybe you want to relive that childish wonder!

(© Credit to Mark for this photo of the Savill Garden. The image displayed has been cropped slightly from the original.)

The Savill Garden

While maybe not “The Obvious”, I still thought this garden worth mentioning. Now this place is beautiful, and I absolutely recommend you visit if you’re around Windsor in the springtime; however, if you pop over to their site, they have a list of season highlights in case you want to know what’s on for the rest of the year.

I have also been to a few of the garden’s outdoor theatre productions, which have been thoroughly entertaining! I suggest you check it out.

National Trust

Some of the most beautiful gardens - make sure to bring a picnic

Cliveden House

I have been to many National Trust places before, and Cliveden is one I keep going back to. The gardens here make for a gorgeous summer day walk, and you can always count on the National Trust for some coffee and cake before you go home.

(My Stepmum says the collection of Dahlias throughout the gardens is awesome, if you're interested in botany!)

The Magna Carta

A lovely place to visit if you’re at all interested in history. Runnymede, where the Magna Carta is located, is mostly known for being the site of this historical signing, providing the foundations for England’s legal system, but the National Trust’s site has more on that.

(© Credit to Steve Johnson for this photo of Hughenden. The image displayed has been cropped slightly from the original.)

Hughenden Manor

Another beautiful place to visit if you enjoy an afternoon stroll with a stop at the end for something sweet to eat and warm to drink.

The Manor itself is stunning, and definitely worth the look inside (most National Trust places will allow you to explore the interior of the buildings).

Bletchley Park

Now, this place is definitely for history lovers. It is absolutely full of fascinating facts pertaining to WW2's brilliant code breakers.

I won’t tell you much, since it was a magnificent experience learning about the code breakers when I came here myself, but here’s their website for reference.

London

Luckily for you, London is only around an hour away from Windsor! There’s plenty to do here - after all, it is the country’s capital city.

I personally have enjoyed going to the theatre here, checking out museums (not just the main ones, there’s a few more eccentric ones in there too!), or hopping over to Camden to explore their endless markets.
